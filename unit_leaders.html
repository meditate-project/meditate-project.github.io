Unit Leaders

Sergio Di Martino

University of Napoli "Federico II"

Sergio Di Martino is Full Professor in Computer Science at University of Naples "Federico II" since February 2022. He graduated in Computer Science at University of Salerno in 2001 and received his Ph.D. in Computer Science at University of Salerno in 2005. His main research interests focus on intelligent transportation systems, empirical software engineering, human-computer interaction, and data mining.

Vittorio Cortellessa

University of L'Aquila

Vittorio Cortellessa has received his M.S. degree in Computer Science from University of Salerno (Italy, 1991), and his Ph.D. degree in Computer Engineering from University of Roma "Tor Vergata" (Italy, 1995). In 1997 he was post-doc fellow at the European Space Agency (ESRIN, Frascati, Italy), working on analysis and evaluation of results in development of technologies for multi-mission ground segment simulation. Since January 1998 through December 1999 he held a post-doc position at DISP, University of Roma "Tor Vergata", working on performance model generation from software artifacts. Since January 2000 through October 2001 he has been Research Assistant Professor at CSEE, West Virginia University. In November 2001 he has moved to University of L'Aquila, where he is Full Professor since March 2018.

Carmine Gravino

University of Salerno

Carmine Gravino is Full Professor at University of Salerno since February 2025. His main research interests regard: management, maintenance, and evolution of software, software measurements' methods, identification of security and privacy aspects during the requirement elicitation phase, and software empirical evaluations. He is a member of the SeSa Lab of UNISA, which focuses on different topics like: software maintenance, evolution, traceability management, testing, and more.
